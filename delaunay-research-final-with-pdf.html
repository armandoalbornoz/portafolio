Our Research on Order-K Delaunay Triangulations

Delaunay triangulations are fundamental geometric structures used extensively in computational geometry, computer graphics, and mesh generation. Their popularity stems from optimal angular properties: standard Delaunay triangulations maximize the minimum angle among all possible triangulations, helping create well-shaped triangles.

Research Question: How can refinement optimize angles in Order-2 Delaunay triangulations? While refinement is well-studied for standard (order-1) Delaunay triangulations, its role in higher-order triangulations remains largely unexplored.

What are Order-K Delaunay Triangulations?

While standard Delaunay triangulations (order-1) use single points as vertices, order-k Delaunay triangulations use k-tuples of points. For order-2, each vertex represents the average (barycenter) of two points from the original set. This creates a more complex structure with interesting angle optimization properties.

The Convex Hull Lifting Algorithm

A key algorithmic contribution of our work is the development of an incremental construction method using the convex hull lifting technique. This approach provides an elegant geometric interpretation of order-k Delaunay triangulations.

Algorithm Overview

The algorithm leverages the classical duality between Delaunay triangulations and convex hulls in higher dimensions. For order-2 triangulations, we lift points to 3D space and compute barycenters, then project the lower hull back to the plane.

1 Lift Points: Project each point (x, y) to 3D using the paraboloid z = x² + y²
2 Compute Barycenters: For all pairs of lifted points, calculate their average (barycenter)
3 Convex Hull: Compute the 3D convex hull of these barycenter points
4 Lower Hull Projection: Identify facets with downward-facing normals and project them back to 2D
5 Triangulation: The projected lower hull faces form the order-2 Delaunay triangulation
Key Challenge:

Computing all O(n²) barycenters becomes computationally expensive for large point sets. Our research explored optimizations using the rhomboid tiling structure to identify only the relevant barycenters that contribute to the lower hull, avoiding unnecessary computation.

Key Findings

01
Angle Optimization
Order-2 Delaunay triangulations extend the local angle property from order-1, lexicographically maximizing the sorted angle vector among all complete level-2 hypertriangulations.
02
Refinement Techniques
We explored Ruppert's algorithm and mesh smoothing for refinement. While both improve angle histograms, order-2 triangulations converge slower than order-1, with blue triangles replicating order-1 structure and red triangles introducing new angles.
03
Algorithmic Efficiency
The rhomboid tiling algorithm significantly outperforms naive barycenter computation. By leveraging the combinatorial structure of hyperplane arrangements, we identify relevant vertices without examining all possible pairs.

Experimental Results

Through extensive experimentation with various point distributions (Gaussian, explosion, convex), we found that:

  • Order-2 triangulations show improvement but retain more small angles than order-1
  • Blue triangles in order-2 replicate order-1 structure, while red triangles introduce new angles
  • The rhomboid tiling algorithm outperforms our initial incremental approach
  • Mesh smoothing provides modest improvements but doesn't eliminate all problematic angles

Future Directions

Our research opens several avenues for future study:

Improved Incremental Algorithms
Developing more efficient incremental construction by identifying relevant barycenters without computing all combinations, possibly using predicted structures from the order-1 triangulation.
Enhanced Refinement
Investigating refinement strategies specifically designed for order-2 triangulations to better eliminate problematic angles, particularly those introduced by red triangles.
Complexity Analysis
Further study of the size and complexity of order-k Delaunay triangulations for k > 2, and whether similar optimization properties hold for higher orders.

Impact: This research contributes to our understanding of higher-order Delaunay triangulations and their potential applications in mesh generation, where avoiding poorly-shaped triangles is crucial for numerical stability and accuracy in finite element methods and computational simulations.
